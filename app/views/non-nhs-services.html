Talking to someone

Although it can be scary or difficult to talk to someone about how you are feeling, it can help you work out what help you need and understand that you’re not alone.

Counselling and online professional support

These organisations provide support from counsellors and other people trained to support children and young people.

ChildLine provides a confidential telephone and online counselling service for anyone under 19 with a problem. You can:

  • call 0800 1111 any time for free
  • have an online chat with a counsellor

The Mix provides a free confidential telephone helpline and online service for anyone under 25 that aims to find young people the best help, whatever the problem. You can:

  • call 0808 808 4994 for free – lines are open from 11am to 11pm every day
  • email question to The Mix
  • get counselling

Kooth is a free, safe and anonymous online mental wellbeing community where you can message or live chat with their support team.

Connecting with others

If you’re struggling with mental health issues, it can help to know you’re not alone and find out how other people manage similar thoughts and feelings.

These organisations provide safe ways for you to get in touch with other young people or read more about other people’s experiences.

  • You can visit Childline’s message boards to ask questions and share experiences
  • The Mix has online group chats and discussion boards
  • Kooth has active and supportive discussion boards
  • You can read other people’s stories on the Young Minds website

Get information and tools to help yourself

It can help to find out more about how are you are feeling and things you can do yourself to help feel better.

  • Every Mind Matters provides information and support, including tips on how to improve your mental wellbeing. Including self-care tips videos for young people.
  • YoungMinds has a wealth of self-help resources on their website, as well as providing dedicated crisis services.
  • The Anna Freud Centre’s On My Mind has a self-care hub for young people.
  • Best for you is on online resources for finding useful mental health apps
