Getting a referral Mental health services and support for children and young people

The first step to getting support from NHS mental health services is usually getting a referral from a professional such as a GP, teacher or social worker.

The referral will give your details to a specialist service that can offer you help, support and treatment.

For some services you may also be able to refer yourself - or your parent or carer can refer you.

A GP can talk to you about what options are available to you in your area and refer you to your local children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS).

Before speaking to a GP, it’s a good idea to think about what to say. You could write down some notes to take with you.

Speaking to a GP about your mental health can seem scary, but they are there to listen to you and help you.

Make sure you tell the GP as much information as you can about

  • how you’ve been feeling
  • how long you been feeling like this
  • how it’s affecting your day-to-day life

It’s really important to be as honest as you can. This will help the GP get a better idea of the support you need.

If you are under 16, you may need your parent or carer’s consent to be referred.

Young Minds is a charity with more information about how to speak to your GP about your mental health on their website

People like teachers, school nurses and social workers can also refer you to children and young people’s mental health services.

You may also be able to refer yourself, or your parent or carer can refer you.

To find out if you can refer yourself to a service, you’ll need to contact your local children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS). The contact details will be on your local service’s website, which you can find by searching online for ‘CAMHS’ and the county or town you live in, for example ‘CAMHS Kent’.

Once you have a referral, or have referred yourself, you should be told how long it will take to hear from the service.

If you do not get this information, it can be helpful to ask so that you know what to expect.

There is likely to be a waiting list, and how long you need to wait will depend on the area that you live in. There is usually a short wait to be contacted for an initial assessment, but there may be a longer wait before you start receiving support.

This can be frustrating if you feel you need support sooner, but there are things you can do to help yourself while you wait.

Most services will try to get back to you within X weeks. If you haven’t heard anything by then, you can try contacting the service. Ask the person who referred you for the contact details, if you don’t have them.

What to do if you’re having difficulty accessing professional support

If you can’t get a referral from your GP, you could try asking a different GP or finding out who else can refer you. It may be useful to know how GPs decide when to refer someone to children and young people’s mental health services.

If you’ve had an initial assessment but have been told that you will not get any treatment or support on the NHS, this can feel disappointing and frustrating.

If a particular service wasn’t able to help, you can try looking for another service that can.

Go back to the person who referred you and ask them what other options there are. It will help if you can tell them why the service said they can’t help you.

You can also do your own research into other services. There is a youth wellbeing directory on the Anna Freud charity website.

If you still cannot get a referral, then there are people who may be able to speak up for you or give you advice. This is called advocacy or being an advocate.

You can find out more about advocacy from:

  • Mind – a mental health charity providing information and support
  • Patients advice and liaison services (PALS) - an NHS service providing confidential advice and support.
