Getting a referral NHS services

The first step to getting support from NHS mental health services is usually getting a referral from a professional such as a GP, teacher or social worker.

The referer will give your details to a specialist service that can offer you help, support and treatment.

For some services you may also be able to refer yourself - or your parent or carer can refer you.

If you are a child or young person

A GP can talk to you, or your parent or carer, about what options are available to you in your area and refer you to your local children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS).

Before speaking to a GP, it’s a good idea to think about what to say. You could write down some notes to take with you.

Speaking to a GP about your mental health can seem scary, but they are there to listen to you and help you.

Make sure you tell the GP as much information as you can about

  • how you’ve been feeling
  • how long you been feeling like this
  • how it’s affecting your day-to-day life

It’s really important to be as honest as you can. This will help the GP get a better idea of the support you need.

If you are under 16, you may need your parent or carer’s consent to be referred.

Young Minds has more information about how to speak to your GP about your mental health

If you are a parent or carer

You can speak to a GP with, or on behalf of, your child. They can give you more information about different types of treatment and services that are available locally. They may want to speak to your child before referring them to CYPMHS but this is not always essential.

Young Minds has more information about how to speak to a GP about your child's mental health

If you can’t, or don’t want to, talk to a GP, there are other people you can ask to refer you to mental health services.

You can ask:

  • a teacher
  • a school nurse
  • a youth worker
  • a social worker, if you already have one

You may also be able to refer yourself, or your parent or carer can refer you.

To find out if you can refer yourself to a service, you’ll need to contact your local children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS).

The contact details will be on your local CAMHS website, which you can find by searching online for ‘CAMHS’ and the county or town you live in, for example ‘CAMHS Kent’. They will usually have a telephone number, email address or form to fill in.

Once you have a referral, or have referred yourself, you should be told how long it will take to hear from the service.

If you do not get this information, it can be helpful to ask so that you know what to expect.

There is likely to be a waiting list, and how long you need to wait will depend on the area that you live in. There is usually a short wait to be contacted for an initial assessment, but there may be a longer wait before you start receiving support.

This can be frustrating if you feel you need support sooner, but there are things you can do to help yourself while you wait.

If you haven’t heard anything after a few weeks, you can try contacting the service. Ask the person who referred you for the contact details, if you don’t have them.

If there is going to be a wait for support, it can be helpful to have people around you that you can talk to who are aware that you are experiencing difficulties.

There are also lots of charities that provide direct support such as counselling and helplines.

Read more about charity and other mental health support.

If your situation changes or gets worse, then call or email the service you were referred to, to let them know what has changed. Or get urgent help if you need it. Self-help techniques can also be a good way to manage your feelings or worries while you wait to be seen.

The charity the Anna Freud Centre has lots of ideas for self-care to help you try to feel better.

If you can’t get a referral from a GP, you can ask a different GP or find out who else can refer you.

It’s important to give the person referring you as much information as you can about how you’ve been feeling.

Information:

It may be useful to know how GPs decide when to refer someone to children and young people’s mental health services.

If you still cannot get a referral, then there are people who may be able to speak up for you or give you advice. This is called advocacy or being an advocate.

You can find out more about advocacy from:

  • Mind – a mental health charity providing information and support.
  • Patients advice and liaison services (PALS) - an NHS service providing confidential advice and support.

You can also do your own research into other services.

  • There is a youth wellbeing directory on the Anna Freud charity website.
  • You can search for local services on the Youth Access website.
