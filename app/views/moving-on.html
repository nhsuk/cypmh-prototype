Moving on Getting help from the NHS

At some point you may move on from getting support from CAMHS.

This might be because:

  • You no longer need the same amount of support
  • A different type of support is more suitable
  • You are over 18 and adult mental health services may be more suitable for you

Moving on from the support you know can be scary and you may need extra help and information during this time.

{% include "includes/urgent-care-card.html" %}

Changing or finishing your support and treatment

If you’re receiving support for your mental health, you’ll regularly talk about your progress and how things are going.

If it’s decided that you no longer need as much support, or need support from a different service, your CAMHS team will help support you to move on (transition).

Moving on to adult mental health services

When you reach 18, and if you still need support for your mental health, your CAMHS team will talk to you about moving on to adult mental health services (AMHS)

This usually happens at 18 but in some areas this transition may happen at 16 or 21.

{% from 'details/macro.njk' import details %} {{ details({ "classes": "nhsuk-expander", "text": "You’ll be given time to prepare", "HTML": "

Your CAMHS team should talk to you about moving on to adult mental health services at least 6 months before you go.

This will help give you time to prepare and ask any questions you have, including:

  • Who will be involved in your care
  • If your family will still be involved
  • Whether there will be a wait for support
" }) }} {{ details({ "classes": "nhsuk-expander", "text": "You’ll make a plan", "HTML": "

If you're moving to Adult Mental Health Services, you should be under something called the Care Programme Approach (CPA).

This means you should have:

  • a care co-ordinator –someone who manages your care and is the person to contact if you have questions
  • a care plan – this says what treatment and support you need, who will provide that support, and the support you might need in a crisis. You should be given a written copy of this plan.

If you're not sure whether you're under the CPA plan, or have not been given a written copy of your care plan, speak to someone in your CAMHS team.

Your CAMHS team should refer you for an appointment with AMHS. If you don’t hear anything, check to make sure this happening.

This appointment is to help work out what support you need, and to see if you are eligible for adult mental health services.

There should be someone from your CAMHS team at this appointment.
