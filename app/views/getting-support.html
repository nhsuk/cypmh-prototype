Getting support Getting help from the NHS

Just as we go to the doctors when we are physically ill, sometimes we need extra help with our mental health.

If you’re going to receive support for your mental health, it can be helpful to know a bit more about what to expect.

When you are referred to CAMHS for support, you will have a conversation about to work out what support is most suitable for you. This might include the following:

Self-management

This is learning ways to manage your mental health by yourself. It is sometimes known as self-care. If your local CAMHS service suggests that you try self-management, they will give you the information and tools you need to support yourself.

YoungMinds has lots of information and ideas to help you look after yourself

Talking therapies

This is where you talk to a professional about your thoughts, feelings and behaviour. There are different types of talking therapies, such as counselling or cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT).

Family therapy

Family therapy is where the members of a family come together to find new ways of dealing with any difficulties, and changes to improve relationships and behaviours.

Creative therapy

Creative therapy is a type of psychotherapy that uses art or other creative activities as a way of communicating difficult, confusing or distressing feelings.

Play therapy is a type of creative therapy used with younger children to help them express how they are feeling.

Medicines

Taking medicine can help some people cope with how they are feeling. Medicines for your mental health are usually only prescribed by a type of doctor called psychiatrist.

The YoungMinds website has lots of information about medicines for your mental health.

Specialist services

CAMHS offers specialist support for some mental health needs, for example if you have an eating disorder or are self-harming. These services may offer a range of therapies, depending on your needs.

Depending on your mental health needs, you may see one or more of the following mental health professionals:

  • child and adolescent psychiatrists – doctors who specialise in children and young people’s mental health
  • clinical psychologists – health professionals trained to support wellbeing and emotional development.
  • child psychotherapists - trained therapists who help children deal with their emotional and mental health.
  • family therapists - trained therapists who work with children and their families together
  • social workers - help children and families needing extra support or help to keep them safe.
  • mental health practitioners

Your local CAMHS team may also include, child doctors (paediatricians), educational psychologists, art therapists, and speech and language therapists.

This will depend on what you are offered. There are a range of places that offer mental health support for children and young people. These include:

  • community CAMHS clinics
  • out-patient clinics or alongside paediatricians in general hospitals
  • specialised in-patient, day patient or outpatient units
  • schools and some GP surgeries
  • social services or youth offending services
  • children’s centres

You may also be able get support at home if you need it.

You will get support for as long as you need it. Sometimes a few sessions will be enough to help you back on track. And sometimes you may need support for longer.

You will always be involved in working out how much support you need.

At some point you may need to move on from getting support. Read more about moving on from getting support.
