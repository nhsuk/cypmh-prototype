  1. Step 1 Getting referred

    To use NHS-funded mental health services, you will either need a referral from a GP, teacher, social worker or youth worker. Or you may be able to refer yourself (or your parent or carer can refer you).

    Find out more about how you can get a referral

  2. Step 2 Working out what help you need

    After you’ve been referred, the mental health service will look at your personal information and think about what support you might need (this is called triage).

    They may get in touch with you, or the person who referred you, to find out more information to help them make that decision.

    You may then be offered an appointment to talk to a mental health professional. Or you may be referred to a different service. You may also be given information and resources so you can do things on your own to help yourself, called self-management.

    Read more about working out what help you need

  3. Step 3 Waiting for support

    If you are referred to NHS-funded services, you may have to wait before you are seen by, or hear from, the service.

    This can be frustrating if you are struggling with how you are feeling, or if you are a parent or carer and want your child or young person to get help sooner.

    There are things you can do while you’re waiting, including:

    • contacting charity services for support,
    • asking about ways to help manage how you are feeling (self-management).
    • asking if there are any digital services available

    Link to new Waiting for support page

  4. Step 4 Getting support

    The support you are offered will depend on what mental health needs you have and how thy affecting your daily life. It might include self-help resources, therapies such as counselling and cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), or medicines.

    Find out more about getting support

  5. Step 5 Moving on

    At some point you may move on from the support or treatment you are getting, This might be because:

    • You no longer need the same support
    • Something has changed and you may need support from a different service
    • You are over 18 and adult mental health services may be more suitable for you

    Moving on from the support you know can be scary and you may need extra help and information during this time.

    Find out more about moving on from CAMHS
