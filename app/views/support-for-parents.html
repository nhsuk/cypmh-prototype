Information for parents and carers Mental health support for children and young people

If you’re worried about the mental health of a child or young person, it can be hard to know what to do to help them. You may also feel like you need support for yourself.

You’re not alone and there are places both you and your child can turn to for help.

It can be difficult to know when and how to get professional support for your child.

If your child is feeling low or anxious for a prolonged period of time, or if you have any serious concerns about how your child is feeling or acting, you may want to seek more professional help.

You may find it helpful to speak to:

  • a GP
  • someone you trust at school or college – for example, a teacher, school nurse or special educational needs co-ordinator (SENCO)
  • a health visitor
  • children's centres

Children and young people’s mental health services (CYPMHS) are NHS services to help people under 18 who are struggling with their mental health.

You may also hear these called children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS).

CAMHS cover a range of mental health issues and provide treatment including talking therapies such as counselling, family therapy and medicines.

A child or young person under 16 will usually need consent from a parent or carer to get help from these services. And they will also usually need to get a referral from a GP, or other professionals working with children such as a teacher, youth worker or social worker.

Read more in our information for children and young people on how to get help from NHS mental health services..

Looking after a child with mental health issues isn’t easy and it’s important you look after yourself as well as your child.

There are a number of organisations you can go to for support and information as a parent or carer.

Support for you

YoungMinds

YoungMinds offers free confidential online and telephone advice and emotional support to anyone worried about a child or young person up to the age of 25.

  • call the free parents' helpine on 0808 802 5544 from 9.30am to 4pm, Monday to Friday
  • email parents@youngminds.org.uk and YoungMinds will respond to your query within 3 working days

Anna Freud

The Anna Freud Centre has an information hub for parents and carers including advice on looking after yourself.

MindEd

MindEd for families is an online e-portal offering free, simple advice about children and young people's mental health for all adults.

Royal College of Psychiatrists

The Royal College of Psychiatrists include mental health information tailored for young people, parents, teachers and carers.

Connect with others

Many parents find it helpful to speak to other parents and carers supporting children with mental health issues.

The PLACE

The PLACE parent support network includes information on local and online support groups.
