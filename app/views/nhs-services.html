If you’re struggling with how you are feeling (your mental health) and feel like you need support, you’re not alone.

The NHS provides specialist mental health services for children and young people who need professional support to manage their mental health.

You may hear these called children and young people’s mental health services (CYMPHS) or children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS).

If you’re a parent or carer of a child or young person who is experiencing mental health difficulties, you can help and support them to access these services.

How do children and young people’s mental health services work?

Services might be slightly different depending on where you live, but they usually involve the following steps:

  1. Step 1 Referral

    To access NHS specialist mental health services, you will usually need a referral from a professional such as a GP, teacher, social worker or youth worker. In some case you may also be able to refer yourself (or your child if you are a parent or carer).

    Find out more about how you can get a referral

  2. Step 2 Triage and assessment

    Once you have been referred, the service will look at your referral information and decide what support you might need (triage). They may get in touch with you, or the person who referred you, to find out more information to help them make that decision.

    You may then be offered an appointment to have an assessment with a specialist. Or you may be given information about self-management resources that could help you.

    Find out more about triage and assessment

  3. Step 3 Treatment

    Your treatment will depend on what mental health condition you have and how it is affecting your daily life but might include talking therapies such as counselling and cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), online self-management support and medication if you want it.

    Find out more about treatment for mental health conditions

  4. Step 4 Moving on to adult mental health services

    When you are turn 18 and if you still need mental health support you will move on to adult mental health services. During this is a period of transition you may need extra support and information.

    Find out more about moving on to adult mental health services
