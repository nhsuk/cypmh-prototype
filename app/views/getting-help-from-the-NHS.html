Getting help from the NHS Mental health support for children and young people

If you’re a child or young person and struggling with how you are feeling (your mental health) you’re not alone.

The NHS provides mental health services for children and young people who need professional support to manage their mental health.

These services are usually for anyone under 18, although you may be able to use some services up to the age of 25.

Support for children and young people

There are different types of support available depending on what help you need and what is available in your local area.

You may be able to get NHS-funded support though:

  • your school or college
  • your local authority
  • voluntary organisations
  • directly from the NHS, through children and young people’s mental health services (CYPMHS).

You can ask your GP, school or college what help is available to you.

How do children and young people’s mental health services work?

Children and young people’s mental health services (CYMPHS) cover a wide range of mental health needs and offer treatment and support including talking therapies such as counselling, family therapy and medicines.

You may more commonly hear these services called children and adolescent mental health services (CAMHS).

There are local CAMHS services around the UK, with teams that include nurses, therapists, psychologists, psychiatrists, youth workers and social workers.
